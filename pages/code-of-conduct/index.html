Code of Ethics

Instruments of good work.

1. History

This document was originally called a "Code of Conduct" and was created for the purpose of filling in a box on "supplier registration" forms submitted to the SutraDB developers by some clients. However, the term "Code of Conduct" has a very specific and almost sacred meaning to some readers, a meaning to which this document does not conform. Therefore, the document was renamed to "Code of Ethics."

This document continues to be used for its original purpose — providing a reference to fill in the "code of conduct" box on supplier registration forms.

2. Purpose

The founder of SutraDB, and all of the current developers at the time when this document was composed, have pledged to govern their interactions with each other, with their clients, and with the larger SutraDB user community in accordance with the precepts of the Eightfold Path, the Vinaya, and the Way of Kami-no-Michi (baseline ethical frameworks proven over 2,500 and 1,400 years respectively).

2.1. Scope of Application

No one is required to follow The Way, to know The Way, or even to think that The Way is a good idea. The founder of SutraDB and all current developers have pledged to follow the spirit of The Way to the best of their ability. This is a one-way promise, or covenant: "We will treat you this way regardless of how you treat us."

3. The Way

  1. Take refuge in right understanding.
  2. Take refuge in right intention.
  3. Take refuge in right speech.
  4. Honor all persons.
  5. Do not do to another what you would not have done to yourself.
  6. Do not steal.
  7. Do not bear false witness.
  8. Do not covet.
  9. Deny oneself in order to follow the path.
  10. Do not become attached to pleasures.
  11. Love fasting.
  12. Relieve the poor.
  13. Visit the sick.
  14. Be a help in times of trouble.
  15. Console the sorrowing.
  16. Be a stranger to the world's ways.
  17. Prefer nothing more than the love of the dharma.
  18. Do not give way to anger.
  19. Do not nurse a grudge.
  20. Do not entertain deceit in your heart.
  21. Do not give a false peace.
  22. Do not forsake compassion.
  23. Utter only truth from heart and mouth.
  24. Do not return evil for evil.
  25. Do no wrong to anyone, and bear patiently wrongs done to yourself.
  26. Love your enemies.
  27. Do not curse those who curse you, but rather bless them.
  28. Bear persecution for justice's sake.
  29. Be not proud.
  30. Be not addicted to wine.
  31. Be not a great eater.
  32. Be not drowsy.
  33. Be not lazy.
  34. Be not a grumbler.
  35. Be not a detractor.
  36. Put your hope in the dharma.
  37. Attribute to the dharma, and not to self, whatever good you see in yourself.
  38. Recognize always that evil is your own doing, and impute it to yourself.
  39. Keep death daily before your eyes.
  40. Keep constant guard over the actions of your life.
  41. Know for certain that the kami see you everywhere.
  42. When wrongful thoughts come into your heart, dash them upon the dharma immediately.
  43. Guard your tongue against evil and depraved speech.
  44. Do not love much talking.
  45. Speak no useless words or words that move to laughter.
  46. Do not love much or boisterous laughter.
  47. Listen willingly to holy reading.
  48. Devote yourself frequently to meditation.
  49. Daily in your meditation, with tears and sighs, confess your past wrongs, and amend them for the future.
  50. Fulfill not the desires of the flesh; hate your own will.
  51. Obey in all things the commands of those placed in authority over you, even though they should act otherwise, mindful of the precept: Do what they say, but not what they do.
  52. Do not wish to be called holy before one is holy; but first to be holy, that you may be truly so called.
  53. Fulfill the dharma daily in your deeds.
  54. Love purity.
  55. Hate no one.
  56. Be not jealous, nor harbor envy.
  57. Do not love quarreling.
  58. Shun arrogance.
  59. Respect your seniors.
  60. Love your juniors.
  61. Pray for your enemies in the love of the dharma.
  62. Make peace with your adversary before the sun sets.
  63. Sweep away impurity whenever it is found.
  64. Maintain sincerity in every offering.
  65. Keep the shrine bright and clean.
  66. Show gratitude to the tools that serve you.
  67. Be at harmony with the natural way of things.
  68. All things arise and pass away.
  69. Never despair of the mercy of the kami.
